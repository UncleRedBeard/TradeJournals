# Trade Journal: Office Restoration

## The Challenge

Document the office and studio work as a preservation-minded room restoration inside the 1894 residence: what existing conditions were uncovered, what repairs or alterations were needed, and how the room moved from guest room to home office to dance studio while keeping its old-house character legible.

## Historic And Building Context

The office belongs in the residence archive as a working-room project where finish quality, comfort, wiring, lighting, and durable surfaces all matter. Its later transition into a dance studio makes the floor work especially important, because the room had to become both visually finished and physically useful.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by phase and trade:

- existing condition and pre-work documentation
- demolition, opening, and investigation
- wall, ceiling, floor, and trim assessment
- electrical, lighting, low-voltage, HVAC, or other system integration
- surface preparation, patching, paint, and finish work
- final room assembly, shelving, furniture, and usability review

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, and reference choices used during the work. Include details such as patching compounds, primers, paint systems, trim profiles, wood repair materials, flooring treatments, lighting components, wiring decisions, and any finish methods that affected the final room feel.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preservation mattered most. Note what was repaired instead of replaced, what had to be modernized for work-room function, and how those decisions balanced daily use, serviceability, comfort, and historic character.

### 2021-12-25 - Office To Studio Floor Refinish

Source: Flickr album [Home Reno - Studio | Office](https://www.flickr.com/photos/boocher/albums/72177720306207693/) and project notes.

This room originally functioned as a guest room and home office before being converted into a dance studio. The project captured in this album tracks that transition and includes the first floors refinished in the house. The result came out especially well and established a successful floor-finishing process for later work.

Floor refinishing process:

1. Strip the polyurethane or varnish top coat with 40 grit sandpaper.
2. Continue with multiple sanding passes using a 5 inch orbital hand sander.
3. Step through 60, 80, 100, and 120 grit sanding passes after the initial 40 grit removal.
4. After completing the room at 120 grit, apply the first coat of water-based polyurethane.
5. Allow the first coat to dry overnight.
6. Sand with 220 grit.
7. Clean, vacuum, prepare the surface, and apply the second coat of polyurethane.
8. Allow the second coat to dry overnight.
9. Sand with 320 grit.
10. Repeat the cleaning, vacuuming, preparation, and application process for the third coat of polyurethane.
11. Sand with 400 grit.
12. Vacuum and steam clean the finished floor.
