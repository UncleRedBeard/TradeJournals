# Trade Journal: Entry Restoration

## The Challenge

Document the entry work as the public threshold restoration inside the 1894 residence: what the first room of the house revealed about use, wear, finish history, and later alteration, and how the repairs protected the house's first impression without stripping away its age.

## Historic And Building Context

The entry belongs in the residence archive as the place where exterior exposure, daily traffic, stairs, trim, doors, floors, and wall finishes meet. Evidence from this area can explain how the house was approached, how circulation was organized, and how previous repairs or finish choices changed the entry over time.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by phase and trade:

- existing condition and pre-work documentation
- finish-layer, paint, and surface investigation
- floor, wall, ceiling, stair, and trim repair
- door, casing, baseboard, threshold, and hardware work
- electrical, lighting, or other system integration
- final finish, cleanup, and quality review

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, and reference choices used during the work. Include details such as sanding methods, patching compounds, primers, paints, floor finish, trim profiles, fasteners, lighting or electrical parts, and any preservation-minded repair method used in the entry.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preservation mattered most. Note what was repaired instead of replaced, what wear or irregularity was retained, and where safety, durability, or daily use required a modern intervention.

## Journal Entries

### 2024-09-14 To 2025-01-04 - Entry, Stair, And Trim Refinement

The first entry sequence concentrates on the house's circulation threshold:
the front door and transom, the stair run, the landing rail, and the adjacent
wall and trim surfaces. Floors were protected with paper and tape before work
around the door and stair, while the door panels, transom, balusters, and newel
post were masked or kept clear during the finish work. The evidence shows the
entry returned to a clean, usable state with its existing door, stair, and
trim relationships intact.

### 2025-12-27 To 2025-12-28 - Entry Floor Finish Renewal

The later sequence records a separate floor-focused pass. It begins with the
entry's darker, worn floor finish exposed between protected walls and the
stair, then ends with a much lighter and more continuous wood surface. The
renewed floor clarifies the entry as a threshold and circulation space while
retaining the white balustrade, dark handrail, front door, and lower wall
paneling that define the room.
