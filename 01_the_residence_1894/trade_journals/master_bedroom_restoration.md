# Trade Journal: Master Bedroom Restoration

## The Challenge

Document the master bedroom work as a preservation-minded room restoration inside the 1894 residence: what original fabric was exposed, what damage or alteration had to be corrected, and how modern comfort could be integrated without flattening the room's historic character.

## Historic And Building Context

The master bedroom belongs in the residence archive as a room-scale record of old-house repair judgment. The work should capture the evidence found during opening, demolition, repair, and finish work, especially where framing, plaster, trim, floors, electrical, or envelope decisions reveal how the house was originally built and later altered.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by phase and trade:

- existing condition and pre-work documentation
- demolition, opening, and investigation
- framing, substrate, plaster, drywall, and surface repair
- electrical, HVAC, insulation, and other system integration
- trim, casing, baseboard, doors, hardware, paint, and finish work
- final room assembly and quality review

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, and reference choices used during the work. Include details such as wall and ceiling substrates, patching compounds, fasteners, primers, paint systems, trim profiles, wood species, sanding methods, and any historic-profile matching.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preservation mattered most. Note what was repaired instead of replaced, what had to be modernized for comfort or code, and how those decisions protected the house's age, proportions, and material feel.
