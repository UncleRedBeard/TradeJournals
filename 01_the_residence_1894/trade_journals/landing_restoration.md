# Trade Journal: Landing Restoration

## The Challenge

Document the landing work as a circulation-space restoration inside the 1894 residence: what the landing revealed about the house's movement paths, floor condition, finish layers, and accumulated wear, and how the repair work preserved that transition space without treating it as leftover square footage.

## Historic And Building Context

The landing belongs in the residence archive because it connects rooms, stairs, trim runs, floor transitions, and daily traffic patterns. Work in this area should preserve evidence of original material choices and later changes, especially where floor repair, finish removal, paint, trim, or threshold decisions affect how the house reads as a whole.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by phase and trade:

- existing condition and pre-work documentation
- finish-layer, paint, and surface investigation
- floor repair, sanding, cleaning, and coating
- trim, casing, baseboard, and threshold repair
- wall, ceiling, and corner surface work
- final cleanup, finish review, and transition details

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, and reference choices used during the work. Include details such as sanding sequence, floor finish system, primers, paints, patching materials, trim profiles, fasteners, hand tools, dust control, and any old-house repair method that affected the final surface.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preservation mattered most. Note what was repaired instead of replaced, where wear was accepted as house history, and where durability or clean transition lines required a more assertive repair.
