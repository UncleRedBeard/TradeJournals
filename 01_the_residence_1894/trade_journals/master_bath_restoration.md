### Early Master Bath Work - Electrical Discovery And Shiplap Lesson

Source: Flickr album [Home Reno - Master Bath](https://www.flickr.com/photos/boocher/albums/72177720306187803/) and project notes.

The master bath project began a long time ago, before Shawn and Haley were married. It became an early turning point in the house work because it was one of the rooms where more serious electrical work started to appear. Opening and working through the room revealed how shoddy some of the previous owner's work, or work hired by the previous owner, had been.

The room also captured an important finish-sequencing lesson: with shiplap, the tongue and groove details need to be cut in before painting. That kind of order-of-operations detail matters because the mistake is easy to hide in planning but expensive to correct once finish work has started.

Key lessons:

- Previous electrical work needed careful inspection rather than trust.
- Electrical corrections became part of the craft record, not just hidden utility work.
- Shiplap prep has to happen before paint.
- Bathroom work exposed the importance of sequencing rough work, substrate work, and finish work.
