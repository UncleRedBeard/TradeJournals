# Trade Journal: Bikes - 2004 KLR 650

## The Challenge

Document the 2004 KLR 650 as a dual-sport machine archive: what condition it
started in, what systems required attention, what made it road- and trail-ready,
and which modifications or maintenance decisions mattered for reliability.

## Historic And Mechanical Context

This motorcycle belongs in the machines archive as a utilitarian dual-sport
counterpart to the vintage scooter and Honda CB projects. Its journal should
track practical service, field readiness, wear items, luggage or accessory
choices, and the kind of maintenance decisions that make a KLR useful rather
than merely presentable.

- Machine: 2004 Kawasaki KLR 650.
- Flickr album: `Bikes - 2004 KLR 650`.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by system and process:

- engine service, oil, cooling, intake, exhaust, and known KLR-specific checks
- frame, subframe, skid protection, racks, luggage, and accessory mounting
- wheels, tires, tubes, brakes, chain, sprockets, and suspension
- wiring, lighting, battery, charging, switches, and trail-side reliability
- tank, plastics, seat, controls, mirrors, and protection hardware
- road-readiness, trail-readiness, safety checks, and maintenance intervals

## Materials And Tools

Record specific tools, consumables, and references as the journal develops.
Include KLR service references, tire and tube tools, chain tools, coolant and
oil details, brake service supplies, electrical test tools, torque references,
and any luggage, rack, or protection hardware notes.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where utility matters more than cosmetic restoration. Note which parts
should stay because they are proven and functional, where upgrades support
reliability, and how each decision affects the bike's role as a working
dual-sport machine.
