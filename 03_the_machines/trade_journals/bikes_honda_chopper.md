# Trade Journal: Bikes - Honda Chopper

## The Challenge

Document the Honda chopper as a custom motorcycle archive: what the machine is,
which major components define its identity, what the starting condition shows,
and how the bike should be understood as a built machine rather than a stock
factory configuration.

## Historic And Mechanical Context

This chopper belongs in the machines archive as a custom motorcycle assembled
from period components.

- Engine: 1974 Honda CB 750 SOHC.
- Frame: 1977 Santee chopper frame.
- Flickr album: `Bikes - Honda Chopper`.

The core preservation question is different from the Vespa. The Vespa journal
tracks factory-era restoration and preservation-minded replacement decisions.
This journal should track a custom build's provenance, component compatibility,
craft decisions, and evidence of how the motorcycle was assembled, maintained,
modified, or repaired over time.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by system and process:

- engine identity, condition, mounts, covers, carburetion, and exhaust routing
- Santee frame condition, welds, tabs, neck area, rear axle plates, and stance
- wiring, lighting, switches, charging, ignition, and battery placement
- front end, rear wheel, brakes, controls, cables, and chain alignment
- fuel tank, seat, fender, paint, chrome, and period-correct details
- safety-critical fasteners, fabrication quality, and repair tradeoffs

## Materials And Tools

Record specific tools, consumables, and references as the chopper journal
develops. Include Honda CB 750 SOHC references, Santee frame notes, fabrication
tools, electrical tools, carburetor tools, chain and alignment tools, chrome
care, rust treatment, and any custom-harness or frame-specific fixtures.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preserving the chopper's custom period character matters most.
Note which parts should stay because they define the machine, where safety or
reliability should override originality, and how any replacement decisions
respect the engine/frame combination.

## Journal Entries

### 2016-07-02 To 2016-07-12 - Chassis Separation And Frame Baseline

The album begins with the motorcycle in a rolling, partly assembled state:
the long front end, fuel tank, seat, rear wheel, and bare 1977 Santee frame
are present while the CB750 engine is set aside. The following days document a
more complete teardown, leaving the frame, rear wheel, front end, and mounting
areas exposed for inspection and later fitment work.

This is the clearest baseline for the custom machine itself. It documents the
relationship between the chopper frame and its intended Honda four-cylinder
engine before reassembly obscures the mounting, stance, and fabrication
decisions.

### 2016-08-14 - Fuel Tank Surface Work

The fuel tank sequence records finish removal and surface preparation. A
maroon-painted tank shows an exposed repair area, followed by a second tank in
bare metal and then a fully stripped, worked surface. The record preserves the
choice to assess the metal directly rather than hide condition beneath an
existing finish.

### 2016-09-04 To 2016-09-29 - CB750 Engine Teardown And Cleaning

The Honda four is documented by subsystem: side covers, alternator or charging
components, carburetors, and associated hardware are separated onto the bench.
The images show a methodical cleaning and inspection stage rather than a
cosmetic before-and-after alone.

### 2018-01-05 To 2018-02-03 - Engine Case Restoration And Top-End Access

Later work focuses on the engine's exterior and upper end. The cases progress
from removed and oxidized parts to cleaned and polished covers. The cylinder
block and cylinder head are then shown off the engine, with the combustion
chambers visible. Together, these photographs establish a continuing engine
refresh rather than a single isolated repair.
