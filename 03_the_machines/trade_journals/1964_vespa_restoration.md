# Trade Journal: 1964 Vespa Restoration

## The Challenge

Document the restoration of the 1964 Vespa as a preservation-minded mechanical project: what condition it started in, what systems required attention, and which original components were worth saving rather than replacing.

## Historic And Mechanical Context

The Vespa belongs in the portfolio as a mechanical counterpart to the 1894 residence. Where the house work preserves historic framing, millwork, and building fabric, the Vespa work preserves pressed-steel body construction, two-stroke mechanical systems, period hardware, and the evidence of original manufacture.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by system and process:

- chassis inspection, rust assessment, and metal repair
- body alignment, panel preservation, and paint decisions
- engine teardown, cleaning, seals, bearings, and reassembly
- carburetor, fuel delivery, and ignition diagnosis
- cables, controls, wheels, brakes, suspension, and road-readiness
- hardware selection, original parts, replacement parts, and tradeoffs

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, consumables, and reference materials used during the work. Include details such as pullers, presses, measuring tools, cleaning methods, rust treatment, sealants, lubricants, and any specialty Vespa tools.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preservation mattered most. Note which parts were restored instead of replaced, where modern replacements were necessary for safety or reliability, and how those decisions respected the machine's age and character.
