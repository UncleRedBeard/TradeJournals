# Trade Journal: Bikes - 1982 Honda CB750 DOHC

## The Challenge

Document the 1982 Honda CB750 DOHC as a machine archive: what condition it
started in, which systems require attention, what should be preserved, and how
the bike's factory-era character should guide repair and maintenance decisions.

## Historic And Mechanical Context

This motorcycle belongs in the machines archive as a later Honda CB750 platform
than the 1974 SOHC chopper engine. Its journal should keep the DOHC machine
distinct from the custom chopper and treat it as its own reference point for
Honda engineering, component layout, condition evidence, and repair planning.

- Machine: 1982 Honda CB750 DOHC.
- Flickr album: `Bikes - 1982 Honda CB750 DOHC`.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by system and process:

- engine identity, condition, covers, seals, carburetion, and exhaust routing
- frame, swingarm, fork, rear shocks, wheels, tires, and brakes
- wiring, lighting, switches, charging, ignition, and battery condition
- fuel tank, seat, side covers, paint, chrome, and trim
- control cables, levers, gauges, fasteners, and rubber components
- safety-critical inspection, road-readiness, and reliability tradeoffs

## Materials And Tools

Record specific tools, consumables, and reference material as the journal
develops. Include Honda CB750 DOHC service references, carburetor tools,
electrical test tools, brake tools, chain and sprocket measurements, tire
information, rust treatment, lubricants, and any model-specific service notes.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where preserving the motorcycle's period character matters most. Note
which original components are worth saving, where modern replacement parts are
appropriate for safety or reliability, and how each choice affects the bike's
identity as a 1982 Honda CB750 DOHC.
