# Trade Journal: Iron Butterfly Cycle Works

## The Challenge

Document the `Iron Butterfly Cycle Works` Flickr album as part of the machines archive: what the album shows about motorcycle work, shop practice, fabrication, repair, or mechanical inspiration, and how those images relate to the broader machine-restoration vocabulary in this project.

## Historic And Mechanical Context

This album belongs in `03_the_machines` because it appears to document motorcycle-adjacent machine work and cycle-shop context rather than a residence or lens-only archive. The journal should preserve what can be learned from the album before deciding whether it becomes a standalone project narrative, a reference album, or supporting evidence for another motorcycle journal.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by machine and shop evidence:

- motorcycle identification, configuration, and condition
- frame, tank, fender, wheel, brake, and control details
- engine, carburetion, exhaust, and drivetrain evidence
- wiring, lighting, charging, switches, and battery placement
- fabrication, welding, brackets, fixtures, and custom details
- shop methods, tools, workholding, staging, and inspection practices

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, and reference choices visible or implied by the album. Include notes on fabrication tools, welding or cutting evidence, paint and metal finish condition, fasteners, electrical tools, motorcycle service references, and any shop setup details that explain how the work was approached.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where safety, reliability, originality, and custom character appear to be in tension. Note what should be preserved as useful craft evidence and what may require later confirmation before treating it as project-specific documentation.
