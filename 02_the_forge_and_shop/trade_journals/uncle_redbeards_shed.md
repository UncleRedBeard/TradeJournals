# Trade Journal: Uncle RedBeard's Shed

## The Challenge

Document `Uncle RedBeard's Shed` as a forge-and-shop archive: what the workspace shows about tool storage, work surfaces, project staging, material reuse, and the practical shop systems that support the broader residence, machines, and lens work.

## Workshop Context

The shed belongs in `02_the_forge_and_shop` because it appears to document the working environment rather than a single finished object. This journal should preserve how the shop is organized, how tools and materials are stored, and how the workspace enables restoration, fabrication, and documentation work.

## Craftsmanship Execution

Capture the work by shop system:

- shop layout, benches, fixtures, and work zones
- tool storage, access, maintenance, and restoration
- material storage, reclaimed stock, hardware, and consumables
- lighting, power, dust control, ventilation, and utilities
- project staging, active work surfaces, and workflow decisions
- safety, cleanup, durability, and long-term serviceability

## Materials And Tools

Record the specific tools, materials, fixtures, and shop systems visible in the album. Include details such as benches, clamps, storage bins, fasteners, shelving, lighting, power strips, hand tools, machine tools, reclaimed wood, metal stock, and any fixtures that support repeatable work.

## Tradeoffs Avoided Or Accepted

Explain where function, durability, cost, reuse, and available space shaped the shop. Note what was built from reclaimed or existing material, what was bought for reliability, and where flexible staging mattered more than a polished finish.

## Journal Entries

### 2016-09-29 To 2018-03-10 - Early Vespa Workbench Evidence

The earliest reviewed shed images establish the workshop as the working base
for the Vespa project. The scooter appears outside the shed, while its engine
and handlebar electrical work occupy a heavy wood bench surrounded by hand
tools, labeled containers, and repair consumables. These images connect the
shed to the machine journal as the place where diagnosis, teardown, and
component-level work happened.

### 2022-01-01 To 2023-02-26 - Shop Layout And Working Systems

The later sequence records the shed as a compact, adaptable workshop rather
than a finished showroom. Corrugated-metal walls, a gravel floor, workbenches,
wall-hung tools, shelving, bins, portable lighting, and active project
fixtures make the available space support several kinds of work at once.
Vespa bodywork and other mechanical parts are staged beside the benches,
showing a practical system built around access, flexible storage, and keeping
ongoing work visible.
